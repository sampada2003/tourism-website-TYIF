Aurangabad


Named after Mughal emperor Aurangzeb, the city
of Aurangabad is speckled with beautiful and
legendary monuments, a legacy of the Mughals,
who reigned from here in 1653. From the marvellous
Bibi-qa-Maqbara, a replica of the world-famous
Taj Mahal, to the formidable Daulatabad Fort,
Aurangabad is steeped in history that is evident
in its glorious ancient structures.
Lying in close proximity to the UNESCO World
Heritage Sites of Ajanta and Ellora Caves,
Aurangabad serves as a good base to explore the
state of Madhya Pradesh. Even though its history
dates back to the earliest periods of Indian
civilisation, its true splendour was unravelled
during the reign of the last great Mughal emperor,Aurangzeb.
